The Airbnb's Of Melbourne

Over the years Melbourne has consistently been ranked as the world's most liveable city. This is no coincidence. Being named the sports capital of Australia, hosting the AFL Grand Final, Australian Open, The Boxing Day Test, and the upcoming T20I World-Cup final. It is the dream location for every sports lover. Not to mention the multiple scenic beaches around Melbourne, the modern infrastructure and easy access to public transport around Melbourne which makes it the ideal location for tourists of all backgrounds. Therefore, the demand for Airbnbs has been increasing to accommodate for tourists who do not always want to stay in hotels.

The following visualisation provides an overview of the information about Melbourne Airbnbs, modelled from data obtained at Kaggle. The visualisations explore the density, price and average size of accommodations across Melbourne, to enable locals and tourists to make well-informed decisions while booking an Airbnb.

The Density of Airbnb's Across Melbourne

The dot density map displays the location of Airbnb's around
Melbourne. The different colours signify the thirty different cities around Melbourne. By hovering over any single data point (Airbnb residence) the city of each Airbnb can be identified.

The map signifies that the central Melbourne area is Airbnb
dense, and as we withdraw from central Melbourne we
observe that the density of Airbnbs also decreases. However, this
trend is only apparent in cities to the East of Central Melbourne.
As the coastline throughout Melbourne is still very dense
and a highly sought Airbnb destination.

Average Price Per City and Land Size

Yarra Ranges is the most expensive city within Melbourne for
travellers, with an average price of $246. On the other end of
the spectrum we observe that the city of Brimbank has the
lowest average price for AirBnBs, $79.

East and South East Melbourne cities have higher average prices
per Airbnb. From the density visualisation, we find that the East
and South East Melbourne cities are also less compact with
respect to central Melbourne. This could be an indication that
the average price is skewed by the soaring prices of a select
few AirBnBs within East and South East Melbourne. In contrast
this signifies that the average price of highly dense areas is
less likely to be skewed.

Average Price Per City and Accomodation Sizes

The majority of AirBnBs which can accommodate at least two
guests are located close to central Melbourne or cities such
as Port Phillip, Bayside and Frankston, cities where the coast
is nearby.

As the filter increases to at least three guests the
visualisation indicates that the price of AirBnBs in the different
Melbourne cities increases with respect to the number of
people, the average Airbnb can accommodate in each city.
The visualisation tends to show a positive correlation
between the price of the average Airbnb and the number of
people, it can accommodate.

However, as the number of people allowed in accommodations increases, this correlation seems to fade away. Evident through the inclusion of Melbourne and Nillumbik as cities that can accommodate on average 3 people. Which otherwise aren't even in the top 7 costly AirBnB Melbourne cities.
