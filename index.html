The Airbnb's Of Melbourne

Over the years Melbourne has consistently been ranked as the world's most liveable city. This is no coincidence. Being named the sports capital of Australia, hosting the AFL Grand Final, Australian Open, The Boxing Day Test, and upcoming T20I World-Cup final. It is the dream location for every sports lover. Not to mention the multiple scenic beaches around Melbounrne, the modern infastrucutre and easy access public transport around Melbounrne which make it the ideal location for tourists of all backgrounds. Therefore, the demand for Airbnb's has been increasing to accomodate for tourists who do not always want to stay in hotels.

The following visualisation provides an overview of the information about Melbourne Airbnb's, modelled from data obtained at Kaggle. The visualisations explore the density, price and average size of accomodations across Melbourne, to enable locals and tourists to make well informed decisions while booking an AirBnB.

The Density of Airbnb's Across Melbourne

The dot density map displays the location of Airbnb's around
Melbourne. The different colours signify the thirty different cities around
Melbourne. By hovering over any single data point (Airbnb residence)
the city of each Airbnb can be identified. Furthermore, the
legend can be used as a filter.

The map signifies that the central Melbourne area is Airbnb
dense, and as we withdraw from central Melbourne we
observe that the density of Airbnbs also decreases. However, this
trend is only apparent in cities to the East of Central Melbourne.
The coastline throughout Melbourne is still very dense
and a highly sought Airbnb destination.

Average Price Per City and Land Size

Yarra Ranges is the most expensive city within Melbourne for
travellers, with an average price of $246. On the other end of
the spectrum we observe that the city of Brimbank has the
lowest average price for AirBnBs, $79.

East and South East Melbounre cities have higher average prices
per AirBnb. From the density visualisation we find that the East
and South East Melbourne cities are also less compact with
respect to centeral Melbourne. This could be an indication that
the average price is skewed by the soaring prices of a select
few AirBnBs within East and South East Melbourne. In contrast
this signifies that the average price of highly dense areas are
less likely to be skewed.

Average Price Per City and Accomodation Sizes

The majority of AirBnBs which can accomodate atleast two
guests are located close to central Melbourne or cities such
as Port Phillip, Bayside and Frankston, cities where the coast
is nearby. As the filter increases to atleast three guests the
visualisation indicates that the price of AirBnBs in the different
Melbourne cities increases with respect to the number of
people the average AirBnB can accomodate in each city.
The visualisation tends to show a positive correlation
between the price of the average AirBnB and the number of
people it can accomodate. However, as the number of people
allowed in accomodations increases this correlation seems
to fade away. Evident through the inclusion of Melbounre and
Nillumbik as cities which can accomodate on average 3 people.
Which otherwise aren't even in the top 7 costly AirBnB Melbourne
cities.
